Prop 19 Explained: How San Diego Seniors Can Transfer Their Tax Base

Quick Answer

California's Proposition 19 allows homeowners 55+ to transfer their property tax base to a replacement home anywhere in California. This can save qualifying seniors tens of thousands of dollars annually in property taxes when downsizing or relocating.

Who Qualifies?

  • Homeowners age 55 or older
  • Severely disabled persons of any age
  • Victims of wildfires or natural disasters

Key Benefits

Under Prop 19, eligible homeowners can:

  • Transfer anywhere in California: No longer limited to specific counties
  • Use up to three times: Previously limited to once per lifetime
  • Buy more expensive: Can purchase a home of greater value (with adjustment)

How It Works: An Example

Current Home: Purchased in 1985 for $150,000

Current Assessed Value: $250,000 (thanks to Prop 13 limits)

Current Property Tax: ~$2,750/year

New Home Purchase Price: $800,000

Without Prop 19: New tax would be ~$8,800/year

With Prop 19: New tax remains ~$2,750/year

Annual Savings: $6,050

Important Deadlines

To qualify for Prop 19 tax base transfer:

  • Must purchase replacement home within 2 years of selling original home
  • Can buy before or after selling
  • Must file Claim for Reassessment with county assessor
